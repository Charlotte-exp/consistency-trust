In this study, we’re exploring how people make cooperative decisions across a series of choices, with some conditions changing each time.

In each of 20 rounds, you’ll choose between two options: “Cooperative” or “Selfish”.

The choices you make will affect your bonus as well as the bonus of a previous participant who participated in this study, as follows:

  • Selfish option: You receive 100 points, while the previous participant receives 0 points.
  • Cooperative option: You receive fewer than 100 points, and the previous participant receives more than 0 points.
The exact points awarded will vary each round.

These points will be converted to cents and added to your and the previous participant’s bonus payments. The “conversion rate” of points to cents will change every round, ranging from 1 to 10 cents per point.

Only one round will be chosen to count toward the final bonus. The chance of any given round being selected varies, ranging from 1% to 10%. Only points from the chosen round will be added to your and the previous participant’s bonus payments.

On each decision screen, you’ll see all the relevant information for that round, including your points, the previous participant’s points, the conversion rate, and the probability that the round will count.
