This is a short experiment on decision-making. In this experiment you will be matched with another participant from Prolific. Neither of you will ever know the identity of the other.

There are two players in this experiment: a Sender and a Receiver.

The experiment is made of two stages: The first stage is made of many rounds of the same task. In each round, the Sender learns about two bonus payment options: A and B. Each option determines a monetary bonus for both participants. With this information, the Sender sends a message about those options to the Receiver. There are two possible sets of bonus payment options. On each round, the Sender can face either one or the other and send the message accordingly.

After the last round is done, one round is selected at random and parameters of that round will be those used in the second stage (bonus options and message). In the second stage, the Receiver receives this message and chooses between Option A and Option B. The message is the only information about the bonuses that the Receiver has. The bonus of both players is determined by the choice of the Receiver.

The Receiver makes the choice, but they do not know the bonuses attached to each options. The Sender does not make the choice, but they do know the bonuses attached to each options.
