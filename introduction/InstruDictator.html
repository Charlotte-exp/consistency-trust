Now we will describe how each interaction works.

At the beginning of each interaction, both the active player and the passive player are allocated an endowment. Both players' endowments will either be {{ Constants.high_half_pot }} or {{ Constants.low_half_pot }}. The size of the endowment is random and might differ between interactions but it will always be the same for the two players.

After receiving endowments, the active player makes the following decision: he/she can leave the endowments as is or take the passive player's endowment. If the active player decides to leave them as is, both players receive their endowment. If the active player decides to take the other player's endowment, he/she will receive both endowments and the passive player will get nothing from this interaction.

Below you can see what the active player's decision will look like if the endowment is {{ Constants.low_half_pot }} in this interaction.

Interaction {{ subsession.round_number }}

Both you and the other player have been endowed with {{ Constants.low_half_pot }} each. You have the opportunity to take the money from the other player, or to leave it.

  • If you take it, your total payoff for this interaction will be {{ Constants.low_half_pot }}.
  • If you leave it, your total payoff for this interaction will be {{ Constants.low_half_pot }}.

During the study, the active player decides by pressing either the "Leave" button or the "Take" button.
