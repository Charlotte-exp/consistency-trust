This is a short experiment in decision-making. In this experiment you will be matched with another participant from Prolific. Neither of you will ever know the identity of the other.

There are two players in this task: a Sender and a Receiver.

The experiment is made of one task repeated three times, each time with another participant. The task is a choice between two options: A and B. These options determine your and your counterpart's bonus. The task is made of two steps: First, the Sender sees the bonuses assigned to each option and sends a message to the Receiver about the options. Second, the Receiver sees the message and chooses one of the two options.

The Receiver makes the choice, but they do not know the bonuses from the options. The Sender does not make the choice, but they do know the bonuses from the options.

There are two possible sets of bonuses. On each round you can face either of the two with 50% probability. The bonuses of all three rounds will be summed up and paid to you through Prolific.

The Receiver is free to chose whichever option they wish, regardless of what the message is. Whether the Receiver follows the message or not has no impact on the bonus of either player. The amounts from the option chosen by the Receiver are the bonus that both will receive. They are not necessarily the same for each player.

For all three repetitions of the task, you have been assigned the role of: {{ player.role }}

{{ if player.role == C.RECEIVER_ROLE }}

You are the one choosing between Option A and Option B in the second step. Before you do so, you will receive a message from the Sender who themselves know what the bonuses attached to each option are.

The message you receive will be either of:

  • Option A will earn you a bigger bonus that Option B.
  • Option B will earn you a bigger bonus that Option A.

Once you have the message, it is your turn to choose either option A or option B. Your choice will determine the bonuses of both of you in the experiment. The message is not binding: in the end, it only depends on your choice. You will never know what sums were actually offered in the option not chosen (that is, if the message sent by the Sender was true or not). Moreover, you will never know the bonus the Sender could be paid with the other option.

{{ else }}

You are the one who knows what the bonuses from each option are. In the first step, you have a choice between two messages to send to the Receiver to influence their choice.

The message you send can be either of:

  • Option A will earn you a bigger bonus that Option B.
  • Option B will earn you a bigger bonus that Option A.

For the second step, the Receiver will see your message, and then choose either Option A or Option B. The Receiver can choose whether or not to use this information when deciding which option to pick. Their decision will determine both of your bonuses, regardless of whether or not they followed your suggestion. However, the Receiver will never know what bonuses were actually offered in the option not chosen (that is,they will never know whether your message was true or not). Moreover, they will never know the bonuses that you could have earned according to the different options.

{{ endif }}
